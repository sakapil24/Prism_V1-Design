Three foundations, resolved

The brief's two reds are kept as written (we'll revisit separation later if it becomes a visual problem). The semantic palette is rebuilt with vivid, lively hues that pass AA on white. The chart palette is fully replaced — no brand-red anchor, no muted muds, just modern, distinguishable colors.

Decision 1 · Reds

Two reds, two jobs (as briefed)

Brand red #D21905 for marketing surfaces and error states inside the product. Product accent #C8102E for every product CTA, active state, and focus indicator. They look near-identical at a glance — accepted for now. If we want to push them apart later, the product accent is the one that should move.

Decision 2 · Semantic palette

Four hues with life in them

Vivid emerald for success, warm amber for warning, clean cobalt for info, brand red for error. None are pale; none are pastel. All four pass AA on white at body size and remain instantly distinguishable from each other at a glance.
